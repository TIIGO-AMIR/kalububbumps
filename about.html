Our History

Kalububbu Muslim Primary School was started way back in 1948 on a land measuring approximately 10 acres donated by the late Haji Muhammad Barifawula and Mr. Kapeera Musa and other elders. It started as a Qur’an school to teach Islam and the basics.

The school started with 15 pupils and a few teachers under the supervision of Haji Nassib Ssekkuunga and Haji Hamza Kateregga.

The first teachers (Sheikhs) included Sheikh Kalangwa Hussein. In 1956, the school was taken over by government to become a government-aided Muslim primary school and remains so to date.

Originally, the school was established to teach Islamic knowledge, but later became a center for academic excellence through both religious and moral education.

Notable persons that have passed through Kalububbu M.P.S include, among others, Haji Nsubuga Ssebbunga, once a district Khadi, Haji Muwanga Kibirige (entrepreneur), and Haji Ssekkuunga, one of the first headteachers.
